## Design Context

### Users
Mixed — established ecommerce operators and early-stage store owners. Neither group is especially data-literate; they need answers surfaced clearly, not raw numbers to interpret. Usage is intentional, not habitual: roughly twice a week to check on store health and act on recommendations. The interface should feel like opening a trusted report, not logging into a tool.

### Brand Personality
**Clean. Premium. Grounded.**
The product handles real money and real business decisions. It should feel trustworthy and polished — like a high-end financial brief, not a startup dashboard. Confidence without intimidation. Clear without being simplistic.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Light mode.** Dark mode is the default for dev tools and "cool" SaaS — Margin Pilot should feel categorically different. A warm off-white base (not clinical white) paired with deep forest green as the accent. Green is semantically correct for a profitability tool and unexpected in the SaaS space. Typography pairs a distinctive editorial grotesque for headings with a clean humanist sans for body copy. Numbers use a monospace face for alignment and precision.

Restrained use of borders and spacing to separate content — not card-boxing everything. Data should breathe. Hierarchy through typography and whitespace, not decoration.

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity over cleverness** — non-technical users need to understand at a glance; no jargon, no clutter
2. **Data with dignity** — numbers should feel significant and legible, not crammed into equal-sized boxes
3. **Earned decoration** — no gradients, glows, or glassmorphism for its own sake; every visual detail earns its place
4. **Warm precision** — this is a financial tool but it should feel like a trusted advisor, not a cold spreadsheet
5. **Report mentality** — each visit should feel like opening a well-crafted brief, not scrolling a feed
